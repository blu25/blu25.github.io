iMat

An interactive floor projection built as a NuVu Studio project. It tracks people's locations and displays their "shadows" on the ground, where they can play with the various toys the iMat has to offer.

Written in Processing

A Kinect generates a depth map of the people in the iMat's space

OpenCV converts the map into blobs, which is used to track position

An EEG brain wave reader is used to change how toys behave

The paint toy's color is modulated by the amount of brain activity
